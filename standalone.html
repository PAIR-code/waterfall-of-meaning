These associations show how people use language, specifically in the news. Word2vec, a ubuquitous ML model used in this visualization, was trained on billions of sentences from news articles. Through this training, it created complex internal relationships between words based on what it saw in the data.

For example, "caviar" and "pizza" aren't just two kinds of food--they have rich cultural associations. When a word crosses an axis in the display, its location shows where it falls on the spectrum - for instance, was "caviar" associated more with the word "cheap" or "expensive" in the dataset?

To this end, any biases in the dataset will be preserved as well. For this reason, it's paramount to ensure that these models are not being used in ways that enforce existing biases, and to continue developing ways to de-bias these models.
